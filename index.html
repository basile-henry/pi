HOW IT WORKS: Pi Approximator uses randomness to estimate what the value of π is.
It uses the fact that the probability for a random point inside the square to be also in the circle is equal to π/4.

  • Acircle = πr²
  • Asquare = (2r)² = 4r²
  • P = Acircle / Asquare = πr² / 4r² = π/4
  • π = 4P

NOTE: This method doesn't give very accurate result since many samples are needed for the estimation of π to look like the π we know. We also have to take into account that the method used for generating the random points isn't truly random.
